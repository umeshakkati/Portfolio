Rohit Sharma – The Hitman of Indian Cricket

Early Life & Background

Rohit Gurunath Sharma was born on April 30, 1987, in Bansod, Nagpur, Maharashtra, India. Coming from a humble background, he was raised by his uncle and grandparents due to financial difficulties. His cricketing journey began at a young age, and he trained under coach Dinesh Lad, who recognized his immense talent and helped him get a scholarship at Swami Vivekanand International School.

Domestic Career

Rohit made his mark in domestic cricket while playing for Mumbai. He first gained attention in the 2006 U-19 Cricket World Cup, followed by a standout performance in the Ranji Trophy, where he scored a double century. His consistency in domestic cricket paved the way for his entry into the national team.

International Debut & Rise

  • ODI Debut: June 23, 2007, vs Ireland
  • T20I Debut: September 19, 2007, vs England (ICC T20 World Cup)
  • Test Debut: November 6, 2013, vs West Indies

Rohit initially struggled to cement his place in the Indian squad due to inconsistency. However, his breakthrough came in 2013 when he was promoted as an opener in ODIs. From then on, he became one of India's most dependable and destructive batsmen.

Records & Achievements

  • ✅ Only player to score 3 double centuries in ODIs (264 vs Sri Lanka, 209 vs Australia, 208* vs Sri Lanka)
  • ✅ Highest individual score in ODIs – 264 vs Sri Lanka (2014)
  • ✅ Most centuries in a single World Cup – 5 centuries in ICC World Cup 2019
  • ✅ Joint-fastest T20I century – 35 balls vs Sri Lanka (2017)
  • ✅ Multiple IPL Titles – Captained Mumbai Indians (MI) to 5 IPL trophies

Leadership & Captaincy

Rohit Sharma took over the captaincy of the Indian team in 2022, leading in all formats. Under his leadership, India won the 2023 Asia Cup and played in the 2023 ODI World Cup final. He is known for his calm and strategic leadership, similar to MS Dhoni.

Personal Life

  • Married to Ritika Sajdeh (sports manager) in 2015.
  • They have a daughter named Samaira Sharma.
  • Passionate about wildlife conservation and is a supporter of Save the Rhino campaigns.
